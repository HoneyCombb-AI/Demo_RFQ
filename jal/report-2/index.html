Assessment
Can Proceed: YesRisk: Medium

16MnCrS5 / 1.7139 is an alloy case-hardening steel within the shop material capability. Pre-heat-treatment cutting is feasible on CNC lathe and gear hobbing equipment; post-heat-treatment hardened surfaces are finished by in-house OD grinding, gear grinding/honing, and inspection. The part envelope Ø11.79 x 61.4 mm easily fits the CNC lathe, sliding-head/CNC turning capacity, gear machines, grinding machines, VMC/five-axis envelope, and heat-treatment capability.

Risks & Mitigations
Post Heat Treat Distortion
The part is case-hardened and tempered to 700+100 HV10 with Eht 0.3+0.2 mm. The small 61.4 mm long shaft with h6/k6/x6 journals and 0.02-0.03 mm runout controls may distort during heat treatment.
Mitigation: Rough/semifinish before heat treatment, retain usable DIN 332 centre holes, then finish OD grind journals and gear grind/hone between centres after heat treatment. Use in-house straightening if required before finish grinding.
Ambiguous Gear Axial Extent
The feature graph records 46.5 mm overall geared/shaft span, 23/23.5 mm segmented dimensions, and 20.9 mm visible/profile length for the helical gear zone.
Mitigation: Use 46.5 mm for setup/cut span and 20.9 mm for active/profile length until clarified; verify on gear CAM and inspection plan before release.
Missing Relief Depths
Depth/profile diameters for the left and right annular reliefs are not fully specified in the extracted feature graph.
Mitigation: Use form-tool relief geometry from released drawing/CAM interpretation before NC release; current route carries width/axial location only.
Tight Fit Classes Not Numeric
ISO fit callouts h6, x6 and k6 are present but the extracted graph does not provide numeric upper/lower deviations. These are tighter than normal turning capability and drive grinding.
Mitigation: Convert ISO 286 fit limits during detailed process engineering and finish grind with in-process gauging.
