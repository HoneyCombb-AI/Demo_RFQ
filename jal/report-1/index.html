Assessment
Can Proceed: YesRisk: Medium

16MnCrS5 / 1.7139 case-hardening steel is within the shop's alloy-steel capability. The Ø39.79 x 14 mm cylindrical gear fits all selected machines. The tightest functional requirements are the Ø9 H7 bore, 0.035 mm gear runout to datum A, DIN 58405 / 8d gear quality, and gear composite/gauge limits; these are achievable in-house using hard finishing machines, but require controlled post-heat-treatment finishing.

Risks & Mitigations
Heat Treat Distortion
The part is case-hardened and tempered to 630+100 HV10 with Eht 0.3+0.2 mm. Heat treatment can distort the Ø9 H7 bore, datum face, and gear flanks.
Mitigation: Leave controlled stock before heat treatment; finish datum faces, bore, and gear flanks after heat treatment using face lapping, ID grinding/honing, gear grinding, and teeth honing.
Gear Profile Definition Incomplete
The extracted feature graph flags an unresolved tooth tip rounding value in the gear data table.
Mitigation: Proceed provisionally using DIN 3972/II basic rack tooling and confirm tooth tip rounding before hob/shave/grind tool finalization.
Surface Finish Interpretation
Rz 2.5 callout may apply to the Ø9 H7 bore or adjacent datum face; the route assumes it applies to the bore.
Mitigation: Finish the bore by ID grinding and honing; confirm callout before release.
Medium Volume Stock Economics
At 700 pieces, a near-net forged blank may be economical for repeat annual production, but no die-cost or long-term volume information is provided.
Mitigation: Use Ø45 mm round bar as the provisional low-risk stock route; re-evaluate near-net forging if annual/repeat production is confirmed.

The part is a small coaxial cylindrical external gear with a central bore, so round bar is the nearest standard form. Although 700 pieces is medium volume, the blank is simple and small; without confirmed repeat volume or forging die economics, Ø42 round bar is the lower-risk provisional choice. Per-piece sawed slug from Ø42 mm round bar. Finished envelope is Ø39.79 x 14 mm; this provides about 2.6 mm radial OD stock and about 6 mm total axial stock for saw cleanup, facing, heat-treat/lapping allowance, and handling. For 700 pieces, process as a batch from long bars with dedicated soft jaws/mandrels.

Route Overview
Family HybridGeometry Cylindrical

The part is a small rotational helical gear. The cost-effective route is to prepare a turned gear blank from round bar, generate the soft gear teeth, deburr before heat treatment, case-harden and temper, then hard-finish the bore, datum faces, and gear flanks to control H7 bore size, runout, surface finish, and DIN gear quality.

  • All selected machine_family values are exact keys from the supplied shop machine_inventory.
  • No outside processes are required; heat treatment, gear cutting, grinding, honing, lapping, finishing, and inspection are all available in-house.
  • Setup time fields are set to 0.0 because setup-time estimation is intentionally not calculated at this planning stage.
  • Round-bar stock is the provisional route for a 700-piece batch. If this becomes repeat annual production, re-evaluate a near-net forged blank using actual die cost and per-piece savings.
  • Final inspection is placed after deburr and cleaning, per manufacturing sequence requirements.
